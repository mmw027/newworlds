City officials confirm that the new transit system is fully operational. However, reports from commuters suggest otherwise.

Instructions provided online do not match in-person experiences. Some passengers report being redirected after following initial guidance.

Updates continue to change throughout the day. No single version of information appears consistent across all platforms.

INFORMATION SHIFTS AS UPDATES CONTINUE THROUGHOUT THE DAY

Passengers reported receiving different instructions depending on the source they relied on. Some followed mobile applications that displayed real-time updates, while others depended on station announcements.

In several instances, commuters described being redirected multiple times within the same journey. Instructions that appeared accurate moments earlier were replaced or contradicted.

Transit workers stated the issue is still under investigation, though updates continue to circulate rapidly online.

“I checked before leaving, but it changed when I got there,” one commuter said. Another noted that platform information displayed on digital boards did not match announcements made at the same location.

Officials stated that these inconsistencies are part of an ongoing adjustment process as the system adapts to real-time conditions. Updates continue to be issued across multiple channels.

Despite these efforts, the pace of change has made it difficult for users to determine which information is most reliable.

TOWN HALL MEETING TOMORROW

City officials will hold a public town hall meeting tomorrow to address ongoing concerns regarding the new transit system.

The meeting is expected to cover inconsistencies in communication, system adjustments, and future updates.

Residents will have the opportunity to raise questions and share their experiences.
